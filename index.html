ZONE 1 — HOLD, STANDARD DCA ONLY
triggerWeekly RSI-14: 50–70
triggerDrawdown: under 5%
Stick to your fixed monthly amount. No extra. No lump sum. Market is fairly valued.
e.g. VOO near ATH, RSI 57 → standard monthly DCA only.
ZONE 2 — INCREASE DCA BY 50%
triggerWeekly RSI-14: 45–55
triggerPullback: 5–10% from ATH
Increase monthly DCA by 50%. Market pulling back but not in crisis.
e.g. MSFT down 5%, RSI 52 → invest $1,500 instead of $1,000.
ZONE 3 — DOUBLE YOUR DCA
triggerWeekly RSI-14: 35–45
triggerPullback: 10–20% from ATH
Double your normal monthly DCA. Historically produces strong 12-month returns.
e.g. META down 12%, weekly RSI 42 → double your monthly allocation.
ZONE 4 — DEPLOY LUMP SUM
triggerWeekly RSI-14: below 35
triggerDrawdown: over 20% from ATH
triggerPrice: below SMA200
Deploy lump sum from cash reserve. Black swan zone. Rare but act decisively.
e.g. Market down 20%, VOO weekly RSI 28 → deploy 30–50% of cash reserve.
RSI-14 vs TradingView — why they must match
This dashboard uses RSI Length 14, Smoothing 1. To match on TradingView: click RSI → Settings → Length = 14, Smoothing Length = 1. Your TradingView was previously set to smoothing 14 which is why NVDA showed 52 there vs 27 here — same stock, very different reading.

Daily RSI-14 = last 14 trading days. Use for DCA timing.
Weekly RSI-14 = last 14 weeks. Use for lump sum decisions.
